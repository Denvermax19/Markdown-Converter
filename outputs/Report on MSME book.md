Academic Evaluation Report on "Garage to Global: The MSME
Playbook"

Prepared By:

Malladi Sreeramaraju, Associate Professor– Department of Management studies
Aditya University.

Positive Aspects of the Book:

1. Comprehensive Coverage of MSME Sector:

The book presents a broad overview of the Micro, Small, and Medium Enterprises (MSME)
ecosystem, particularly from an Indian perspective. It attempts to guide the reader through
various phases of business growth, from local establishment to global scaling.

2. Macro-Financial Insights:

It touches upon critical financial and policy-related topics relevant to established businesses.
The discussion includes regulatory frameworks, credit access, and market linkages, which are
valuable for experienced entrepreneurs.

3. Case-Based References and Practical Scenarios:

Through its industry-centric approach, particularly in sectors like food processing and textiles,
the book shares real-life challenges and practical experiences, offering insights to those already
operating in these domains.

4. Resource Suggestions:

The book suggests various digital tools such as Hootsuite, Mailchimp, and SEMrush, which are
industry-standard platforms for marketing, automation, and analytics.

Limitations and Concerns:

1. Overly Broad and Advanced for Students:

The content leans heavily toward macroeconomic policy, managerial strategies, and high-level
operational concerns. These topics may overwhelm beginner-level students or early-stage
startup founders who seek foundational guidance in entrepreneurship.

2. Outdated and Expensive Tool Recommendations:

The tools suggested (e.g., SEMrush, Hootsuite, Mailchimp) are not only expensive but also less
suited for lean startups or student-led ventures. More modern, budget-friendly alternatives like
Beehiiv, Brevo (formerly Sendinblue), or Buffer are not considered.

3. Lack of Clarity on Startup Fundamentals:

The book fails to provide a structured approach for identifying a business opportunity,
validating an idea, or developing a minimum viable product (MVP). Critical areas such as
ideation, prototyping, lean validation, and funding for beginners are not addressed.

4. Limited to Traditional Industry Focus:

The examples and guidance primarily cater to legacy sectors such as food processing and
textiles. There is minimal to no coverage of emerging sectors like technology, SaaS, climate-
tech, or creator economy—areas highly relevant to today's student entrepreneurs.

5. Lack of Global Perspective:

The book remains narrowly focused on Indian markets and does not equip readers to think
beyond domestic opportunities or adapt to global entrepreneurial trends.

Final Recommendation and Conclusion:

While Garage to Global: The MSME Playbook is a commendable effort in documenting MSME-
related experiences and offering guidance for traditional, established businesses, it lacks relevance for
the current era of student entrepreneurship. Its focus on capital-intensive models, outdated tools, and
traditional sectors make it less effective for students seeking agile, innovation-driven startup guidance.

Conclusion:
This book may serve as a supplementary resource for seasoned business professionals or MSME
owners, especially in legacy sectors. However, it is not recommended as a primary reference book
for entrepreneurship students at our institution. For our curriculum—which emphasizes innovation,
digital-first business models, lean startups, and global scalability—other more contemporary and
student-friendly texts would be better suited.

Recommended Alternatives:

For a more student-centric and globally relevant approach to entrepreneurship, the following books are
highly recommended:

• Zero to One by Peter Thiel – For insights on creating innovative, monopoly-style businesses

from scratch.

• The Lean Startup by Eric Ries – For a practical, iterative approach to starting and scaling

businesses with limited resources.

• Harvard Business Review Entrepreneur’s Handbook: Everything You Need to Launch
and Grow Your New Business – A comprehensive and accessible manual covering startup
planning, funding, marketing, and growth strategies, curated by industry experts.

These titles better align with our institution’s commitment to fostering innovation-driven, globally-
minded, and resource-conscious entrepreneurs.
